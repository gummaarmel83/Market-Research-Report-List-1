<p><ul><li>Low Viscosity Cements</li><li>Medium Viscosity Cements</li><li>High Viscosity Cements</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The spinal cement system market is categorized into three main types based on viscosity: low, medium, and high viscosity cements. Low viscosity cements flow easily, allowing for better penetration into fractures but may not provide optimal support for larger voids. Medium viscosity cements offer a balance between flow and stability, making them suitable for various applications. High viscosity cements are thicker, providing increased strength and resistance to leakage, ideal for stabilizing vertebral compression fractures and ensuring lasting support during spinal procedures.</p></p>

<p><p>The spinal cement system market primarily revolves around two key applications: vertebroplasty and kyphoplasty. Vertebroplasty involves the injection of bone cement into fractured vertebrae to stabilize and alleviate pain, enhancing mobility and quality of life for patients with osteoporosis or trauma. Kyphoplasty, on the other hand, incorporates a balloon to create space before cement injection, restoring spinal height and correcting deformities. Both procedures aim to provide effective treatment for vertebral compression fractures, improving patient outcomes and recovery.</p></p>
