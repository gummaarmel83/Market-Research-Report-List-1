<p><p>The Anti-nuclear Antibody (ANA) Testing market includes several types of diagnostic methods. Indirect Immunofluorescence is a classic technique where patient serum is incubated with substrate cells, revealing antibodies under fluorescence microscopy. Enzyme-linked Immunosorbent Assay (ELISA) is a more standardized, quantitative method that utilizes enzyme-linked antibodies for detection. Other methods may include rapid tests, bead-based assays, and multiplex assays, each offering varying sensitivity and specificity in diagnosing autoimmune disorders, contributing to a diverse and evolving testing landscape.</p></p>
